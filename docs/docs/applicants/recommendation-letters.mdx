# Recommendation Letters

Some doctoral positions require one or more letters of recommendation. On TUMApply you nominate
your recommenders, and they submit their recommendation directly through a secure link, so you do
not need to collect or forward any documents yourself. This page explains how to add recommenders,
keep their submissions confidential, and monitor their progress.

:::info[Only shown when a position requires it]
The **Recommendation Letters** step appears only for positions that require recommendations. The
professor decides, per position, how many recommendations are required and whether recommenders
provide a letter, a structured evaluation, or both.
:::

---

## ✍️ Adding Your Recommenders

The **Recommendation Letters** step is part of the application form. For each recommender, enter
their details and select **Add**.

<Image src={referencesStep} alt="Recommendation Letters step of the application form" size={ImageSize.large} caption="Recommendation Letter Step"/>

**For each recommender, provide:**

- **Title** — optional (for example, *Prof. Dr.*, *Prof.*, or *Dr.*)
- **First Name** — required
- **Last Name** — required
- **Email** — required, and used to send the secure invitation link

Below the form, the **Recommenders** section shows **Required** (how many recommendations the
position needs) next to **Added** (how many you have added so far), so you can see your progress at
any time.

:::note
Invitation emails are sent when you **submit your application** — not when you add a recommender.
Until submission, your recommenders are saved but not yet contacted.
:::

---

## 🔒 Keeping Recommendations Confidential

At the bottom of the step you will find the option:

> *I waive my right to access the submitted recommendations. Only the supervisor can read them.*

- This option is **selected by default**.
- When **selected** (confidential), only the supervising professor can read the submitted
recommendations. You will still see who you asked and whether they have submitted, but not the
content.
- When **cleared**, you will also be able to view the submitted recommendations once they arrive.

The setting applies to the **whole application** — that is, to all of your recommenders — rather
than to each recommender individually.

:::tip
Many recommenders expect their assessment to remain confidential and may provide more candid
feedback when it is. Keeping the waiver selected is common practice.
:::

---

## 📊 Tracking Status

Each recommender in the list shows a status, so you can see the current state of each request:

- **Requested** — the invitation has been sent and the recommender has not responded yet.
- **Submitted** — the recommendation has been received.
- **Declined** — the recommender chose not to provide a recommendation.
- **Expired** — the deadline passed before the recommender submitted.

---

## 🛠️ Managing Recommenders After You Apply

You can **add, edit, or remove** recommenders even after submitting your application, from your
application's detail page.

A few conditions apply:

- Changes are possible only while a request is still **pending** (**Requested**) and
while the application is still active and the deadline has not passed.
- Once a recommender has **Submitted**, **Declined**, or the request has **Expired**, that entry
can no longer be edited or removed.
- Editing a recommender's email address sends a **new invitation** to the updated address.

---

## 📨 What Your Recommenders Will Do

Your recommenders do not need a TUMApply account. Each receives an email with a **secure link** and
completes the request there:

- The page shows who the request is for — your name, the position, and the research group — and
whether the recommendation is confidential.
- Depending on the position, they will **upload a recommendation letter** (PDF), **complete a short
structured evaluation** (their relationship to you, an assessment of you compared with peers, and
an overall recommendation), or **both**.
- They can also **decline** the request.
- The link remains valid until the position's deadline (max. two months), and **reminder emails**
are sent automatically as the deadline approaches.

---

## 🚪 Withdrawing Your Application

If you withdraw your application, you receive a confirmation email and the application is marked as
**Withdrawn**. Recommendations that have already been submitted are unaffected.

Recommenders whose requests are still **pending** are notified by email that their recommendation
is no longer needed, so that no open request remains for an application you have withdrawn.

---

:::tip[Add recommenders early]
Because invitations are sent when you submit and recommenders may need time to respond, please add
your recommenders and submit your application well before the deadline.
:::
